LITERATURE LANDSCAPE CHAPTER 5  ·  LITERATURE

literature  ·  framing vs methods  ·  where this thesis sits

Two columns, five clusters.

Methods drive the thesis; framing gives it vocabulary. The right column is the body of work this research builds on — surrogate-assisted MOEAs, generative-design workflows, building-performance applications. The left column is small but load-bearing: CAS appears as the interpretative lens through which the methods cohere, not as a formal core.

framing  ·  theoretical lens

CAS as vocabulary.

Three anchors. CAS is a lens — it gives names (adaptation, feedback, emergence) to behaviour the methods produce, not a formal mechanism the methods implement.

  • Holland 1995

    Addison-Wesley · book · CAS canon

    Hidden Order — origin of the adaptive-agents framing and the trio adaptation / non-linearity / aggregation that this thesis re-uses.

  • Mitchell 2009

    Oxford UP · book · modern survey

    Complexity: A Guided Tour — accessible synthesis of CAS, used here to ground emergence terminology without going formal.

  • Batty 2007

    MIT Press · book · CAS in built-environment

    Cities and Complexity — closest precedent for applying CAS vocabulary to physical-design domains, the bridge from CAS to architecture.

Scope decision: CAS as interpretative lens, not theoretical core (see slide 02 — open positioning).
