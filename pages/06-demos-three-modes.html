Three runs, one problem.

Same multi-objective test problem, three coupling regimes — a pure evolutionary run with no surrogate, an offline regime where a pre-trained model replaces real evaluations, and an online regime where the surrogate is refit as the search proceeds.

Note: each frame change = one simulation. Incident-radiation evaluation takes ~3 s per call under the original Ladybug pipeline; here it is GPU-accelerated to a fraction of that. The videos are sped up 10× overall, and pure mode is now refreshing too fast to render correctly.

claim  ·  what the three runs show
With 10% of the pure-mode evaluation budget, the surrogate already produces a credible front — and the MOEA has not yet converged. Offline pays its cost up front in a single large-sample fit; if the model is a tree ensemble or a neural net, the per-call inference cost is no longer effectively free and must be traded against the saved real evaluations. Online matches offline's speed once θ is frozen, and reaches a strictly better front by refitting on the infill the MOEA actually visits.
