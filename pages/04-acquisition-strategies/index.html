method  ·  infill selection — eight strategies, two routes

Choosing the next K real-sim points.

Every N generations the framework hands K population members to the true simulator. Two families of acquisition decide which K — one inherited from Bayesian optimisation (algorithm-agnostic, μ + σ² only); one native to SAEA, borrowing non-dominated rank and crowding from the MOO host. Both consume the same context object.

A·1  ·  explore–exploit

UCB

score = μ − κσ (min)

Simplest signal: σ-large means explore, μ-small means exploit. κ is hand-tuned, no Bayesian inference.

Srinivas 2010 / 2012 — GP-UCB regret bounds

A·2  ·  bayesian classical

EI

score = 𝔼[max(f* − f, 0)]

Closed-form under Gaussian posterior. Adds a Bayesian layer over UCB — probability of improvement × magnitude.

Jones 1998 — efficient global optimisation

A·3  ·  mo → so scalarisation

ParEGO

score = EI(Tchebycheffλ)

K weights λ → K candidates → natural batch. Implementation reuses multi-output (μj, σ²j) — q-ParEGO style, no per-infill GP retraining.

Knowles 2006  ·  Daulton 2020 q-ParEGO

A·4  ·  hypervolume integral

EHVI

score = 𝔼[ΔHV(y; PF, ref)]

Most rigorous MO acquisition. m=2 closed-form O(N); m=3 box decomp.; m≥4 falls to Monte Carlo. Baseline uses MeanOnObserved (NoisyEI consistency).

Emmerich 2005  ·  Daulton 2020 q-EHVI

Gen 0 fallback

NSGA hasn't done a selection round — ranks unreliable, σ² noisy. Both routes degenerate to maximin distance in variable space: pick K candidates farthest from the existing training set. Classic LHS-style space-filling.

MaximinAcquisition.cs
claim
Bayesian acquisition is what BO ships; Variance + MOO is what the host MOO uniquely enables — rank and crowding are signals BO simply doesn't have. Both routes fit the same context; the framework leaves the choice to the user, with mixed as the default.
