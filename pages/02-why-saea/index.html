WHY SAEA CHAPTER 3  ·  MOTIVATION

motivation  ·  surrogate-assisted MOO vs Bayesian optimisation

Why surrogate‑assisted MOO, and not BO / TPE.

Bayesian optimisation already bundles surrogate + acquisition + search into a self-closed loop, so the question is not does the surrogate need updating — BO updates too. The question is which search engine drives the loop, and where each engine collapses.

premise A — evaluation

A single simulation is expensive.

EnergyPlus, Radiance, CFD, FEM: minutes to hours per call. Both BO and SAEA exist because of this.

premise B — search

Engineering search is high‑d, many‑m, batched, mixed.

HPC farms parallelise; performance objectives stack to 4–6; sliders are quantised. BO's acquisition optimiser breaks here.

axis 01  /  batch size

The q wall.

q = candidates per real-sim batch

BO / TPE

Standard EI emits q = 1. q-EHVI O(q!) in the integrand; BoTorch concedes >q ≈ 10 already stretches a single acquisition call.

SAEA (this work)

A GA generation is a batch. N=100 evaluated in parallel, infill K=5–20 selected from the live population — no batch-aware acquisition needed.

Daulton 2020 q-EHVI, NeurIPS[pdf] BoTorch docs on q-EI scaling cf. InfillStrategy.cs mixed 60/20/20

axis 02  /  objective count

The m wall.

m = number of objectives

BO / TPE

EHVI closed-form only at m=2; m=3 needs O(N²) box decomp.; m≥4 falls to Monte Carlo, noisy acquisition surface, L-BFGS unstable.

SAEA (this work)

NSGA-III, MOEA/D, RVEA documented up to m=15. Reference vectors and niche-decomposition replace the integral entirely.

Emmerich 2005 EHVI Deb & Jain 2014 NSGA-III[pdf]

axis 03  /  variable space

The mixed-variable wall.

d quantised, discrete, conditional

BO / TPE

Acquisition optimised by L-BFGS + multi-start — assumes continuity. TPE's KDE degrades past d ≈ 30.

SAEA (this work)

SBX + polynomial mutation handle continuous, integer, categorical in one operator family. Slider quantisation is a post-hoc projection, not a constraint.

Bergstra 2011 TPE limits Deb 2002 SBX[orig] cf. OptimizerComponentBase.Online.cs:130

claim
Surrogates solve evaluation scarcity; MOO operators with non-dominated ranking solve search scarcity. BO bundles both jobs into one engine — and that engine breaks at the q, m, and mixed-variable thresholds that engineering optimisation crosses on day one.
